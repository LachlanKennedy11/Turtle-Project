Status: Threatened.

Description: Most common nester in AL. Known for its **large head** and powerful jaws. Shell is reddish-brown.

Diet: Primarily **carnivorous** bottom feeder, crushing hard-shelled prey like **mollusks and crustaceans**.

üè° Habitat & Alabama Presence

Habitat: Highly adaptable, found in offshore waters, **bays, lagoons, and salt marshes**.

In AL: Nests regularly, though sparsely, on Alabama's coastal beaches from **Dauphin Island to the Florida border**.

ü•ö Nesting & Movement

Nesting: Solitary, **night-time nesters**. Lay 3-5 nests per season (approx. 100 eggs each).

Status: Endangered (U.S. and State). **Alabama's Official State Reptile.**

Description: Large freshwater turtle. Distinctive feature is its **bright red or orange plastron** (bottom shell).

Diet: Almost entirely **herbivorous**, feeding on submergent aquatic plants.

üè° Habitat & Alabama Presence

Habitat: Shallow, vegetated **freshwater and brackish backwaters** with soft, muddy bottoms.

In AL: Restricted to the **Mobile-Tensaw River Delta** in Mobile and Baldwin Counties.

ü•ö Nesting & Movement

Nesting: Nests on dry, sandy land near the delta. Lay a small clutch of **4 to 9 eggs**. Hatchlings may **over-winter** in the nest.

Tracks: Leaves a **wide path** on sandy banks leading from the water to the nest site, with wide foot and tail drag marks.
